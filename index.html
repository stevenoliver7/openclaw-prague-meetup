What is OpenClaw? 🤔

OpenClaw is an open-source AI assistant framework that runs on your own machine.

Think of it as your personal AI sidekick — one you can customize, extend, and control completely. It connects to your tools, reads your files, manages your calendar, and helps you build automations that actually fit your workflow.

Unlike closed AI assistants locked behind corporate APIs, OpenClaw is built for tinkerers. Run it locally. Connect it to anything. Share your skills with the community. Make it yours.

What makes it special:

• Skills system — Add new capabilities by writing simple markdown files
• Multi-model support — Run Claude, GPT, Gemini, or local models
• Tool integrations — GitHub, Gmail, Jira, calendar, browser automation, and more
• Privacy-first — Your data stays on your machine

The Vibe 🎯

Community-first AI event with strong open-source energy.

OpenClaw is about the freedom to design new systems and being playful with AI. Eventually, frontier labs will squeeze the joy out of it — but right now we can have a ton of fun with open-weight models.

This is for builders, thinkers, and tinkerers. Not for people who want to generate AI slop and get rich — this is about curiosity, craft, and having fun.

📋 The Agenda

1. Free Talk & Introductions

Open floor — meet the people around you. Casual networking.

2. Structured Discussion Round

Guided questions to share with the group:

• What's your setup?
• What are your skills?
• How long have you been using OpenClaw?
• Which models do you run?

A Miro board will be opened so everyone can pin up their answers for a collective, structured discussion.

3. Energy Round

Shooting ideas, sharing setups, producing energy together.

4. 2 × Short Keynotes (~30 min each)

Inspiring talks from people who went deep — built something that genuinely saves time, improves a business, or is just really cool.

📣 Submissions open for two keynote slots!

5. Hands-On Building Session

Bring your laptops, Mac Minis, or whatever you run OpenClaw on. The goal: everyone leaves having built or improved something.

📦 Contribute to the Community Repo

A public GitHub repo where attendees can share skills, ideas, and configurations.

How to contribute:

  1. Fork the community repo
  2. Create a folder with your name (e.g., contributors/your-name/)
  3. Push your skills, SKILL.md files, configs, or anything useful inside
  4. AI will be run over all submissions to add structure and make them easier to share

Contributions welcome before the event — if you have something to add, submit a PR!
