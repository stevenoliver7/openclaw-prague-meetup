What is OpenClaw? 🤔

OpenClaw is an open-source AI assistant framework that runs on your own machine.

Think of it as your personal AI sidekick — one you can customize, extend, and control completely. It connects to your tools, reads your files, manages your calendar, and helps you build automations that actually fit your workflow.

Unlike closed AI assistants locked behind corporate APIs, OpenClaw is built for tinkerers. Run it locally. Connect it to anything. Share your skills with the community. Make it yours.

What makes it special:

• Skills system — Add new capabilities by writing simple markdown files
• Multi-model support — Run Claude, GPT, Gemini, or local models
• Tool integrations — GitHub, Gmail, Jira, calendar, browser automation, and more
• Privacy-first — Your data stays on your machine

4. 2 × Short Keynotes (~30 min each)

Inspiring talks from people who went deep — built something that genuinely saves time, improves a business, or is just really cool.

📣 Submissions open for two keynote slots!

5. Hands-On Building Session

Bring your laptops, Mac Minis, or whatever you run OpenClaw on. The goal: everyone leaves having built or improved something.

📦 Contribute to the Community Repo

A public GitHub repo where attendees can share skills, ideas, and configurations.

How to contribute:

  1. Fork the community repo
  2. Create a folder with your name (e.g., contributors/your-name/)
  3. Push your skills, SKILL.md files, configs, or anything useful inside
  4. AI will be run over all submissions to add structure and make them easier to share

Contributions welcome before the event — if you have something to add, submit a PR!

❓ FAQ

Do I need to bring a laptop?

For the hands-on building session — yes! If you want to participate in building, bring your laptop, Mac Mini, or whatever you run OpenClaw on. If you just want to listen and network, no problem — come as you are.

Is this for beginners or advanced users?

Both! We'll have people who just discovered OpenClaw yesterday and people who've built complex automations. The structured discussion round helps everyone share their level and learn from each other.

What if I don't have OpenClaw installed?

Come anyway! Part 5 (Hands-On Building) is perfect for getting set up. Bring a laptop and we'll help you get running. Bonus: you'll leave with your first skill built.

Is there food/drink?

We're aiming for a pub venue, so yes — beer, wine, and food available for purchase. This isn't a catered event, so bring cash/card. The vibe is "tinkerers in a pub," not "conference with lukewarm coffee."

Can I present something?

Yes! We have 2 keynote slots open for ~30 minute talks. DM us on Telegram or email with your topic. Also, the Energy Round is open mic style — anyone can share.

How do I get there?

Prague has excellent public transport. Metro lines A, B, C all connect to the center. Most venues we're considering are within 10 min walk of a metro station. We'll announce the exact location by March 10.

Is it in English or Czech?

Both! Daniel speaks both fluently, and the community is international. Main presentations will be in English, but smaller discussions can be in whatever language works.

🚇 Getting to Prague

From Václav Havel Airport (PRG)

Take the Airport Express bus to Nádraží Veleslavín (15 min), then Metro A to the center (10 min). Total: ~30-40 min, ~$3.

Or take a taxi/Uber for ~$15-25 to the center (20-30 min depending on traffic).

From Main Train Station (Hlavní nádraží)

Right in the center! Metro C, trams, all connect here. 5-10 min walk to most central venues.

Public Transport in Prague

Excellent metro, trams, buses. Single ride ~$1.30, day pass ~$5.50. Get the PID Litacka app for easy ticket purchase.
